## Executive summary

A serious modern giving and contributions platform is no longer just a donation form attached to Stripe. Across current church-giving, donor-management, and fundraising products, the recurring pattern is a full operating system for contributions: public giving pages, one-off and recurring gifts, donor or supporter self-service, fund and campaign structure, receipts and statements, payment-state reliability, reconciliation, exports, and role-based admin controls. Planning Center, Donorbox, Tithely, Pushpay, ChurchSuite, Breeze, and Bloomerang all expose some version of that full loop rather than treating ŌĆ£payment succeededŌĆØ as the end of the journey. For GetFlow, the most important product decision is to position the app as a **multi-organisation, Stripe-native giving platform with light donor CRM capabilities** rather than as a generic checkout wrapper. That means the core system needs to be organisation-scoped from the database upwards, supporter-aware rather than payment-only, and reliable enough that admins can trust totals, receipts, recurring schedules, and payout reconciliation. SupabaseŌĆÖs row-level security model is well suited to tenant isolation, and StripeŌĆÖs own guidance strongly favours webhook-driven state handling for payment completion and subscription lifecycle changes. Before real communities use GetFlow, three things must be true. First, **money flow reliability** must be boring and dependable: every Checkout session, webhook event, recurring schedule, failure, retry, refund, and payout must be traceable and reconcilable. Stripe recommends webhook-based status handling, idempotent requests for safe retries, and explicit recovery for undelivered events; the established giving platforms all expose payout, transaction, or bank-deposit reporting because finance teams need this.
